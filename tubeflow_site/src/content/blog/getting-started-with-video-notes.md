Taking notes while watching videos can transform passive viewing into active learning. With TubeFlow, you can capture insights at the exact moment they happen, making it easy to revisit key concepts later.

## Why Timestamped Notes Matter

Traditional note-taking often loses context. When you review your notes days or weeks later, you might wonder what the speaker was saying when you wrote a particular point. Timestamped notes solve this problem by linking your thoughts directly to the video timeline.

## How to Take Better Video Notes

### 1. Watch First, Then Note

Don't try to capture everything on your first viewing. Watch a section to understand the context, then rewind slightly and add your note at the key moment.

### 2. Use Your Own Words

Paraphrasing helps you process information more deeply. Instead of transcribing what the speaker says, write what it means to you.

### 3. Capture Questions Too

If something isn't clear, note your question at that timestamp. You can come back to it later or search for additional resources.

### 4. Create Action Items

When a video inspires you to do something, create a note marked as an action item. TubeFlow makes it easy to filter these later.
